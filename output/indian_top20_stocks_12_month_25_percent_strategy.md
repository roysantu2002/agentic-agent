PART B — Valuation framework & selection methodology
Valuation approach (how we pick targets & bands)
1) Universe & filters
   - Initial: Top 500 listed Indian companies (NSE/BSE).
   - Quality filters: 3–5 year ROE preferably >15% or a demonstrable path to >15%; revenue & EPS growth positive; improving FCF conversion; reasonable leverage; interest coverage >4x for industrials; clean governance.
2) Valuation overlay
   - Two-pronged: (A) multiple-justified value vs peers (P/E, EV/EBITDA, P/B) and (B) catalyst-driven re-rating over 12 months (deal wins, monetisation, margin recovery).
   - Seek margin of safety: trade inside accumulation bands (entry bands) and use DCF/justified-multiple checks where appropriate.
3) Final selection
   - Balanced mix: core large-caps for stability, mid-caps for growth, select small-caps for alpha. 20 names selected to be diversified across sectors with conviction ranking.

Valuation signals used to set entry bands & targets
- Use consensus FY27–FY28 EPS and reasonable multiple compression/expansion scenarios.
- Entry band = accumulation zone where risk/reward acceptable (usually 5–15% below current market or within recent support).
- 12‑month target = realistic blend of earnings growth + modest multiple expansion (or catalyst-driven re-rating) aiming at portfolio-level upside near target.

PART J — Entry, exit and profit-booking framework (actionable rules)
Entry rules (execution)
- Use staggered buys (3–5 tranches) within entry band to average cost and reduce timing risk.
- Place limit orders within stated accumulation ranges (entry band).
- Avoid full position at a single price; use tranche sizes: 40% / 30% / 30% or 25% increments if using 4 tranches.

Profit-booking / exit rules
- Primary profit booking: Trim 20–40% of a position at target price or when stock reaches +25–35% above cost (depending on size/weight).
- Reallocation: rotate proceeds into underperformers that still meet fundamentals or keep cash.
- Stop-loss: Predefine stop-loss levels (12–20% for large caps; 10–15% for mid caps). Use mental or actual orders depending on liquidity.
- Trailing stop strategy: convert to trailing stop when position reaches +15–20% profits; e.g., 12% trailing stop from peak.

Event-driven exits
- Immediate exit (or heavy reduction) triggers:
  - Governance red flag (insider selling >2% or auditor resignation)
  - 3 consecutive quarter earnings misses or material order cancellations
  - Significant deterioration of asset quality (for financials)
  - Unexpected regulatory or legal events that materially impact business.

PART K — Monitoring framework & quarterly review checklist (operational)
Quarterly checklist (for each stock)
- Revenue & EBITDA: growth vs consensus and prior quarter trend.
- Margins: gross, EBITDA, and net — watch 2‑quarter directional changes.
- Cash flow: FCF and working capital swing (trend).
- Balance sheet: net debt movement, leverage ratios, interest coverage.
- Banks/NBFCs: GNPA, NNPA, PCR, stage-2 exposure, restructuring %.
- Orderbook / backlog (L&T, BEL): gross order inflow change.
- Deal pipeline (TCS/Infosys): large deal wins or cancellations.
- Product & capacity updates (Divi’s, Ultratech): timelines and utilization.
- Promoter / institutional trades: >2% changes flagged.
- Governance alerts: auditor changes, management exits, related-party deals.
- Market signals: sector ETF flows, FII/DII flows, currency moves (INR).

Quarterly actions
- If a position >12% of portfolio, trim to target weight.
- If a stock drops 15–25% but fundamentals intact, consider topping up with max 25% of available cash.
- Rebalance to target weights quarterly; apply tax-aware harvesting annually (realize 10–15% gains in winners).

PART L — Rebalancing rules & execution notes
- Quarterly rebalancing is mandatory: trim winners and top-up losers within fundamental guardrails.
- Rebalance triggers:
  - If a holding exceeds target weight by >50% (e.g., target 6% becomes 9%), trim to target.
  - If a holding drops below 50% of target weight and fundamentals intact, top up to target in tranches.
- Tactical rebalancing:
  - Use cash buffer for opportunistic buys during market dips (>5–7% market correction).
  - Employ limit orders; avoid market orders in low-liquidity names.

PART M — Risk management & hedging (portfolio-level)
Hedging considerations
- For portfolios >₹25L: buy Nifty put spreads (cost-efficient) to cap tail risk during uncertain macro.
- For targeted protection: use single-stock puts on large holdings if concentrated >10% weighting.
- Alternatives: use call overwrites on positions where you expect minor upside and want yield (income generation).

Concentration & liquidity rules
- No more than 12% in a single stock (unless one has highly risk-tolerant mandate).
- Ensure minimum 5% uninvested cash for quick entries on dips.
- For mid-cap holdings, maintain smaller position sizes and daily monitoring.

- Tactical tilt suggestions (choose one approach depending on risk appetite):

1) Moderate tilt (recommended for aggressive-but-controlled)
   - Increase weight in high-upside names: raise Infosys & Bajaj Finance from 8% → 10% each.
   - Increase ICICI from 6% → 8% and UltraTech / Eicher each from 3% → 5%.
   - Fund by reducing defensive staples (Nestle/HUL) by 1–2% each.
   - Expected incremental effect: portfolio expected return moves toward mid-23% to 25% depending on realized earnings.

2) High-conviction concentrated tilt (higher risk)
   - Move to a 12 stock concentrated portfolio: overweight high-upside cyclicals/financials (Infosys, Bajaj Fin, ICICI, UltraTech, Eicher, BEL, Dr. Reddy’s) at 8–12% each and reduce defensive staples weight.
   - This can lift expected outcome to ~25%+ BUT increases volatility, drawdown risk and idiosyncratic exposure — only for high risk-tolerance investors.

3) Leverage/Option overlay (institutional)
   - Keep base portfolio but purchase an out-of-the-money call spread or buy call options on 20–30% notional of portfolio concentrated in names where you expect large catalysts (Infosys, Bajaj Fin, UltraTech). Limited cost can generate outsized upside.
   - Or sell covered calls for income and buy put protection for downside.

Choose the tilt that best matches your risk tolerance and liquidity needs.
